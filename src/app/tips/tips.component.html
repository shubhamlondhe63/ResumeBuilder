ATS-Friendly Resume Tips

Master the art of creating resumes that pass through Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) and reach human recruiters. Follow these expert tips to increase your chances of landing interviews.

What is an ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software used by employers to collect, scan, and rank job applications. Over 95% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS to filter resumes before they reach human recruiters.

Important: If your resume isn't ATS-friendly, it may never reach human eyes!

Formatting Essentials

  • Use simple, clean fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
  • Stick to standard sections (Experience, Education, Skills)
  • Use bullet points, not paragraphs
  • Save as .docx or .pdf (check job posting preferences)
  • Avoid headers, footers, and text boxes

Keyword Optimization

  • Mirror job posting language exactly
  • Include both spelled-out and abbreviated terms
  • Use industry-specific terminology
  • Include relevant technical skills
  • Use action verbs (achieved, managed, developed)

ATS Do's

Use Standard Job Titles

Stick to conventional titles like "Marketing Manager" instead of "Marketing Rockstar"

Include Contact Information

Put your phone, email, and location at the top of your resume

Quantify Achievements

Use numbers and percentages to show your impact

Include Education Details

List degree type, major, school name, and graduation year

ATS Don'ts

Avoid Images and Graphics

ATS can't read images, logos, or complex graphics

Don't Use Tables or Columns

Complex layouts can confuse ATS scanning

Avoid Creative Fonts

Fancy fonts may not be readable by ATS

Don't Keyword Stuff

Use keywords naturally, not in hidden white text

Test Your Resume

Before submitting your resume, test its ATS compatibility:

Quick ATS Test Methods:

  • Copy and paste resume text into plain text editor
  • Check if information is still readable and organized
  • Use online ATS scanning tools

What to Look For:

  • All text is present and readable
  • Sections appear in correct order
  • No scrambled or missing information
